Our Journey

  • October 2012

    We meet in Paris

    Darinka has just moved to Paris to start her Master’s degree, while Henrik has taken up a position as a postdoctoral researcher. Both of us live on the same campus, the Cité Universitaire. One day, we meet in our residence’s laundry room: Henrik is struggling with the dryer and Darinka comes to his rescue. Following a short conversation, Darinka finds out that Henrik lives in a much bigger room than she does and expresses an interest to check it out. Henrik may have interpreted her curiosity somewhat differently …

  • 12 January 2013

    We start dating

    Since our initial meeting, we have been hanging out regularly, exploring Parisian culture. Our paths part for Christmas break. Henrik goes home to Celle to relax with his family and friends, and Darinka visits her family in Koblenz. She spends most of her time studying for her upcoming exams in January. The only light at the end of the tunnel is a phone call that Henrik promised to make. If she had not taken the initiative, she would probably still be waiting. As history shows, things turn out differently: We officially start dating when we get back to Paris in January.

  • Summer 2013 - Summer 2015

    We travel the world together

    One of our favorite activities is to explore other countries. A first big holiday takes us to Israel and Jordan in August 2014. Henrik shows Darinka the city of Tel Aviv where he worked for two years and takes her to the City in the Rocks, Petra, one of the new seven wonders of the world. The following year Darinka gets the opportunity to share with Henrik the part of the world she used to live in for several years. During a trip to the U.S., we visit Chicago (IL), Washington D.C., Lexington (VA), and Durham (NC).

  • October 2015

    We move in together

    After having lived in Lille for a year and coming back to Paris on the weekends, Henrik suggests that we move in together: A colleague of his has told him about a direct connection from Lille to Saclay (where Darinka works). Taking this train, Darinka could live in Lille and commute to her lab every day – spending only 3 hours on the train. To the colleague’s dismay, it turns out that it is faster for Henrik to commute from Paris to Lille, taking only 2 hours round-trip. Henrik therefore moved into Darinka’s apartment, located at the foot of Montmartre, a former artists' quarter.

  • 23 August 2016

    We get engaged

    Travelling in northern Italy to attend a friend’s wedding, Henrik has designated the Forte di Belvedere in Florence as the location. Or not? On the morning of the decisive day, a last-minute google search reveals that Kanye West and Kim Kardashian had picked the Forte for their own wedding, placing their tacky altar in the very spot Henrik has envisioned. He changes his mind. After hours of visiting the Palazzo Pitti, the adjacent gardens, and the Forte di Belvedere, he finally proposes: not to Darinka (as suspected by her), but rather to take a walk along the Arno River to the Piazzale Michelangelo. At sunset, we make it into the rose garden, just in time for him to pop the question. The flare of the moment is briefly interrupted by an unwitting tourist who decides to unwrap her sandwich in our very spot. Darinka quickly scares her away, say yes, and then witnesses yet another return of the sandwich-eating klutz.

Venue

Our ceremony, reception, and party will be held in the Obere Mühle, a former mill, located in Rengsdorf, in the foothills of the Westerwald - a mountain range near Koblenz. Situated in the middle of a forest, it provides a gorgeous view over one of Germany's largest rhododendron parks.

The main building and terrace: The reception will take place here, allowing us to enjoy the terrific view of the park.

The park: If weather permits (fingers crossed, it's Germany after all), the actual ceremony will take place here.

The "Stadl": This traditional dining hall offers a panoromic view of the surrounding park. Dinner will be served here.
